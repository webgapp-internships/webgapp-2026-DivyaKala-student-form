History of Nature & Beauty

Nature has been an important part of human life since the beginning of civilization. Early humans depended on forests, rivers, mountains, and animals for food, shelter, and survival. Over time, people began to admire the beauty of nature and its peaceful surroundings.

Ancient cultures around the world considered nature sacred and respected the environment. Trees, flowers, rivers, and the sun were often worshipped because they were believed to represent life, strength, and harmony.

During the Renaissance period, artists and poets started expressing the beauty of nature through paintings, literature, and music. Landscapes, gardens, and natural scenery became symbols of peace and inspiration.

In modern times, people have become more aware of the importance of protecting nature. Environmental movements encourage planting trees, saving wildlife, reducing pollution, and keeping the Earth green and healthy.
